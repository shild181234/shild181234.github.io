About me

I received my PhD degree from Tsinghua University, where I worked on water and sediment science, river engineering, and hydro-thermodynamic processes in regulated river-reservoir systems.

My previous research focused on hydro-thermal processes, stratified flows, confluence mixing, reservoir regulation, and turbulence in large channel-type reservoirs. My current research at the University of Toronto examines winter processes in lakes, including under-ice thermal structure, dissolved oxygen dynamics, benthic heat release, and the influence of thermal gradients on cold-water fish habitat use.

Research

Winter Limnology

I study under-ice thermal structure, inverse stratification, benthic heat release, radiatively driven convection, and seasonal transitions in dimictic lakes.

Oxygen Dynamics

My work examines how winter conditions regulate dissolved oxygen storage, redistribution, and ecological memory effects that influence spring and summer oxygen conditions.

Fish Habitat Use

I investigate how winter thermal gradients and oxygen conditions shape the depth distribution and habitat use of cold-water fish in seasonally ice-covered lakes.

Reservoir Hydrodynamics

My previous work focused on hydro-thermal processes, stratification, mixing, turbulence, and phytoplankton transport in large channel-type reservoirs.

Stratified Flows

I am interested in stratified flows, gravity currents, confluence mixing, and their roles in transporting heat, oxygen, sediment, and ecological materials.

Climate Change Impacts

My research explores how changing ice cover, snow cover, warming, and wind conditions affect lake thermal regimes, oxygen dynamics, and aquatic ecosystems.
