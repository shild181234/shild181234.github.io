Physical Limnology, Winter Lake Processes, and Aquatic Habitat Dynamics

I am a postdoctoral fellow in the Department of Physical and Environmental Sciences at the University of Toronto. My research focuses on physical limnology, winter limnology, dissolved oxygen dynamics, lake thermal structure, and fish habitat use in seasonally ice-covered lakes.

I am broadly interested in stratified flows in reservoirs, lakes, and rivers, particularly mixing dynamics, thermal stratification, hydrodynamic processes, and their ecological implications for oxygen dynamics, phytoplankton distributions, and aquatic habitats.

About me

I received my PhD degree from Tsinghua University, where I worked on hydro-thermodynamic processes in regulated river-reservoir systems.

My current research at the University of Toronto examines winter processes in lakes, including under-ice thermal structure, dissolved oxygen dynamics, and the influence of thermal gradients on cold-water fish habitat use. I am especially interested in understanding how winter climate variability affects lake physical and ecological processes.

Research

Winter Limnology

I study under-ice thermal structure, inverse stratification, benthic heat release, radiatively driven convection, and seasonal transitions in dimictic lakes.

Oxygen Dynamics

My work examines how winter conditions regulate dissolved oxygen storage, redistribution, and ecological memory effects that influence spring and summer oxygen conditions.

Fish Habitat Use

I investigate how winter thermal gradients and oxygen conditions shape the depth distribution and habitat use of cold-water fish in seasonally ice-covered lakes.

Reservoir Hydrodynamics

My previous work focused on hydro-thermal processes, stratification, mixing, and turbulence in large channel-type reservoirs under climatic and hydrological forcing.

Stratified Flows

I am interested in stratified flows, gravity currents, confluence mixing, and their roles in heat, oxygen, sediment, and ecological transport.

Climate Change Impacts

My research explores how changing ice cover, snow cover, warming, and wind conditions affect lake thermal regimes, oxygen dynamics, and aquatic ecosystems.
